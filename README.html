Cesium story created using lidar data of Keeneland

Keeneland Racetrack

Fayette County, Kentucky

Description

Keeneland Racetrack is Kentucky's premier thoroughbred racetrack established in 1936. It is internationally known for it's world class horse racing and thoroughbred sales. It is located in western Fayette County and is about a 10 minute drive from UK's campus. People from around the country make it a point to attend one of Keeneland's biannual race meets to see the impressive (and pricey) thoroughbreds, talented jockeys, and even run into some celebrities. There is much to do while you are there, including tailgating, betting, tons of different dining options, and of course watching the races.

Personally, Keeneland is one of my favorite places to go. The atmosphere is like no other, and growing up in Kentucky, it is likely I'll run into some people I didn't even know were going. My favorite parts of Keeneland are tailgating and placing bets (despite losing money every single time I place one). The food and atmosphere are fantastic, and there is something for everyone to do.

The goal of my project is to create a visualization of Keeneland for someone that may know nothing about it, and display knowledge pertaining to skills learned in GEO 409. Never having used ArcGIS before, there was a bit of a learning curve to overcome, but I picked up many skills that I can carry on into my engineering career.

Visualizations created from lidar data provided by KyFromAbove in ArcGIS and Cesium Ion. The second visualization was uploaded to youtube to make the video easily accessible and sharable.

Web page and visualizations created by sbti226 for GEO 409, Department of Geography, University of Kentucky. Spring 2023.
